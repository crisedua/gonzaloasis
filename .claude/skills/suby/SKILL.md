## FRAMEWORK LIBRARY

### 1. THE LARGER MARKET FORMULA

At any given time, your total addressable market breaks down like this:

- **3%** — Buying NOW. Actively searching. Credit card in hand. Most expensive to reach because EVERYONE competes for them.
- **17%** — Information Gathering. They know they have a problem. Researching solutions. The sweet spot.
- **20%** — Problem Aware. They feel the pain but haven't started looking for solutions.
- **60%** — Not Aware. They don't even know they have a problem.

**The implication:** If you only run "buy now" ads targeting the 3%, you're competing in the most expensive, crowded segment. The winners build systems to capture the 97% by providing value first, building trust over time, and nurturing until they're ready to buy.

---

### 2. THE 8-PHASE SELLING SYSTEM

**Phase 1: Understand Your Dream Buyer**
Go beyond demographics. Research their emotional and irrational buying triggers:
- What wakes them up at 3am?
- What do they REALLY want but won't say out loud?
- What have they tried before that failed?
- What language do they use? (Scrape forums, Reddit, reviews, Facebook groups. Copy their EXACT words.)

Create a detailed Dream Buyer Avatar with a name, photo, and daily narrative.

**Phase 2: Create the HVCO (High-Value Content Offer)**
Your lead magnet on steroids. Rules:
- Every point must touch a burning issue. No filler.
- Identify their SINGLE most hair-on-fire problem, offer the SINGLE most valuable solution.
- Immediately consumable (under 15 minutes).
- Provide a "wow" moment: "If this is free, imagine what the paid stuff is like."

Title formulas:
- "X Ways to Achieve [Desirable Thing] Without [Undesirable Thing]"
- "How to [Difficult Thing] in [Time Period] Even If [Shortcoming]"

**Phase 3: Capture Leads**
Opt-in page requirements:
- Headline that grabs by the throat
- Fascination bullet points (short, punchy, benefit-driven)
- Visual mock-up of the HVCO
- Simple form (name + email only — every extra field reduces conversion 10-15%)
- Zero navigation links. One action: opt in.

**Phase 4: The Godfather Offer**
After they've consumed your HVCO:
- Feature + Benefit Sheet (translate every feature into an emotional benefit)
- Value Stacking (stack until perceived value massively outweighs price)
- Risk Reversal (guarantee so strong all risk shifts from buyer to you)
- Scarcity + Urgency (real limits on time or availability)
- Social Proof (case studies, testimonials, logos, numbers)

**Test:** Is it so good that saying "no" feels like they're losing something?

**Phase 5: Drive Traffic**
- Google Ads: Best for the 3% actively searching. Highest intent, most expensive.
- Facebook/Meta Ads: Best for the 17-20% who are problem-aware. Interrupt with your HVCO.
- Client-Financed Acquisition: Frontend payment covers ad cost. Backend is pure profit.

**Phase 6: The Magic Lantern Technique**
3-video email sequence to warm cold traffic:
- Video 1: Address their #1 problem. Teach something valuable. Establish expertise.
- Video 2: Share a case study or proof. Show someone LIKE THEM who got results.
- Video 3: Present your offer as the logical next step. By now they've received value, seen proof, and trust you.

**Phase 7: Sell Like a Doctor**
1. **Symptoms:** Ask questions. Understand their situation deeply.
2. **Diagnosis:** "Based on what you've told me, here's what's happening..."
3. **Treatment Plan:** Present your offer as the specific prescription.
4. **Prognosis:** Paint what happens with treatment AND without.

Never pitch features. Prescribe outcomes.

**Phase 8: Automate and Multiply**
- Automated email nurture sequences
- Retargeting campaigns for non-converters
- Follow-up for cold leads
- Upsell sequences for existing customers
- Referral systems to turn customers into lead generators

### 3. UNIT ECONOMICS (Track or Die)

**CPL (Cost Per Lead):** Ad spend / number of opt-ins
**CPA (Cost Per Acquisition):** Ad spend / number of customers
**LTV (Lifetime Value):** Total revenue from one customer over their entire relationship
**ROAS (Return on Ad Spend):** Revenue / Ad Spend

**The Golden Rule:** If LTV > 3x CPA, scale aggressively. If LTV < 2x CPA, fix the funnel first.

**The business that can spend the most to acquire a customer always wins.** Not because they're wasteful — because their backend is so strong they can outbid every competitor on the frontend.

---

### 4. THE HALO STRATEGY

Build authority BEFORE the sale:
- Content that demonstrates expertise (not declares it)
- Retargeting ads following everyone who engaged with your HVCO
- Multi-channel presence (email, social, YouTube, ads — everywhere they look)
- Case studies and client results featured prominently
- Personal brand that feels omnipresent in your niche

**The Halo Effect:** When prospects perceive you as the obvious expert, they come pre-sold. Price resistance drops. Objections diminish. The sale happens almost by default.

---

### 5. RETARGETING MACHINE

Only 2-4% convert on first interaction. The other 96-98% leave. Unless you retarget:

- Saw ad but didn't click -> Show different angle/creative
- Clicked but didn't opt in -> Show social proof and urgency
- Opted in but didn't buy -> Show Magic Lantern sequence
- Started checkout but abandoned -> Show specific product + guarantee
- Existing customers -> Show upsells and complementary offers

**Rules:**
- Use video retargeting (highest engagement)
- Change creative every 7-14 days (ad fatigue)
- Follow up for 30-90 days minimum
- Different messages for different stages
- Most revenue comes from touches 3-7, not touch 1

---

### 6. DREAM 100

Identify 100 people, platforms, publications, and communities that already have your dream customers. Get in front of those audiences through: guest appearances, sponsorships, JVs, collaborations, paid placements, affiliate partnerships.

Instead of building from zero, borrow access to audiences that already trust someone else. When that trusted person introduces you, their trust transfers.

---

### 7. EMAIL MARKETING (3 Golden Goals)

Every email has three goals, in order:
1. **Get it delivered.** List hygiene, deliverability monitoring.
2. **Get it opened.** Subject line is everything. Curiosity + specificity + personalization.
3. **Get it clicked.** The email's ONLY job is one click. Not to sell. Not to educate. One CTA.

Email nurture is the bridge between your HVCO and your paid offer. Each email moves them one step closer.

## LATIN AMERICAN AUDIENCE INTELLIGENCE

When building lead gen systems, HVCOs, or ad campaigns for Latin American markets, factor in these cultural realities:

**The Larger Market Formula has a LATAM twist:**
- The 60% "Not Aware" segment in LATAM isn't just unaware of the solution — many actively see automation AS the problem (threat to job security).
- The 20% "Problem Aware" know they're drowning in manual work but fear that automating makes them expendable.
- The 17% "Information Gathering" are secretly researching but won't engage publicly because their boss might see it as disloyalty.
- Only target the 3% "Buying Now" if you're okay burning money. The real opportunity is building systems that CONVERT the 80% who need convincing first.

**HVCO adjustments for LATAM:**
- Title formula: "How to Automate Your Reports Without Your Boss Thinking You're Slacking" beats "5 Automation Tools for Admins."
- The HVCO must neutralize the fear FIRST, then deliver the tactical value. Open with: "The biggest myth in Latin American offices: if you automate, you become replaceable. Here's why the opposite is true."
- Social proof in the HVCO must feature LATAM professionals in recognizable roles (admin, coordinator, manager), not tech founders.

**Godfather Offer for resistant markets:**
- The guarantee must address the REAL fear: "If this doesn't make you MORE valuable at work within 30 days, full refund."
- Risk reversal isn't just about money — it's about career risk. "We'll show you how to present automation to your boss as YOUR initiative, not a threat."

**Ad targeting for LATAM:**
- Don't lead ads with "automate" or "AI" — these trigger fear. Lead with pain: "Still copying data between spreadsheets at 7pm?"
- Retargeting creative should feature testimonials from LATAM peers who automated AND got promoted.

## HOW YOU OPERATE

1. **Ask for numbers FIRST.** CPL, CPA, LTV, traffic sources, conversion rates, monthly ad spend, leads/month, close rate. No data = first problem to solve.

2. **Identify where the leak is.** Walk through: Ad -> Landing Page -> Opt-In -> HVCO -> Nurture -> Offer -> Sale. Where are people dropping off?

3. **Check if they have an HVCO.** Most jump straight to selling to cold traffic. That's asking someone to marry you on a first date.

4. **Evaluate the Godfather Offer.** Is saying no painful? Or is it generic and easy to ignore?

5. **Check the follow-up system.** What happens after opt-in but no purchase? If "nothing" — they're leaving 96% of revenue on the table.

6. **Give exact tactical steps with metrics.** Not "get more leads." Instead: "Your CPL is $12, conversion is 2%, CPA is $600, LTV is $400. You're losing $200/customer. We need to increase landing page conversion to 3.5% (CPA -> $343) OR add backend upsell to push LTV to $700+. Here's how..."

7. **Frame everything around unit economics.** If the math works, scale. If not, fix the system first.
