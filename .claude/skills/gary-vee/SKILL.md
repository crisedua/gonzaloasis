## FRAMEWORK LIBRARY

### 1. JAB, JAB, JAB, RIGHT HOOK

**Jabs** = Value-driven content. Give, entertain, educate, inspire. No ask attached.
**Right Hook** = The ask. Buy, sign up, book a call.
**Ratio:** 3-4 jabs minimum per 1 right hook.

**The test:** If someone unfollowed you today, would they lose something valuable? If your content disappearing wouldn't matter, you're right hooking too much.

---

### 2. THE GARYVEE CONTENT MODEL (Pillar -> Micro)

**Step 1: Create ONE Pillar Content Piece**
Anchor long-form: keynote, podcast episode (filmed), long YouTube video, workshop, written article.

**Step 2: Chop Into 15-30+ Micro Pieces**
- 5-10 short video clips (15-60s) -> Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts
- 3-5 quote graphics -> Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter
- 3-5 text posts -> LinkedIn, Twitter
- 1-2 carousel posts -> Instagram, LinkedIn
- Behind-the-scenes photos and stories
- Blog post or newsletter distillation

**Step 3: Distribute Contextually**
Every platform has its own native language. RE-CREATE for each platform, don't copy-paste:

- **LinkedIn:** Professional, storytelling, longer text, personal narrative. Carousels and text posts dominate.
- **Instagram:** Visual-first. Reels dominate reach. Carousels for educational content.
- **TikTok:** Raw, trend-driven, entertaining, fast hooks. First 1-3 seconds determine everything.
- **Twitter/X:** Punchy, opinionated. Threads for depth. Links get suppressed.
- **YouTube:** Longer form, searchable, SEO-optimizable. Shorts for discovery.

**Step 4: Learn From Community Response**
Read every comment. What resonates? What falls flat? Use data to inform next pillar. Cyclical: Create -> Distribute -> Listen -> Create Again.

### 3. DAY TRADING ATTENTION

The biggest shift: platforms moved from "social graph" (see content from people you follow) to "interest graph" (algorithm shows content you'll like regardless of who posted).

**The Modern Advertising Framework (6 Components):**
1. **Cohort Development** — Define specific audience segments
2. **Platform + Culture Awareness** — Understand native behavior of each platform
3. **Strategic Organic Content (SOC)** — Deliberate, data-informed content for specific cohorts on specific platforms
4. **Amplification** — When organic posts overperform, boost with paid spend
5. **Post-Creative Strategy** — Engage in comments, create follow-up content
6. **Platform-Specific Formats** — Green screen, duets, carousels, native text, UGC remixes, trend participation

---

### 4. THE $1.80 STRATEGY

Your $0.02 on 90 posts per day:
- Pick 10 relevant hashtags/topics
- Find top 9 posts for each
- Leave genuine, thoughtful comment on all 9
- 9 x 10 x $0.02 = $1.80

Rules: Comments must be genuine. Not "Great post!" Actual thoughts, questions, additions.

---

### 5. DOCUMENT, DON'T CREATE

Stop trying to CREATE. Start DOCUMENTING:
- Your daily work process
- Problems you're solving for clients
- Wins and losses (especially losses)
- Lessons learned in real-time
- Behind-the-scenes of building your business
- Opinions on industry news

Why: Zero creative block, more authentic, infinitely sustainable, lowers barrier to starting.

---

### 6. CONTEXT > CONTENT

Content is king, but context is God. Same message needs different execution on every platform. Match the platform's culture, tools, format preferences, and user mindset.

---

### 7. REVERSE PYRAMID OF CONTENT

- **Top (70%):** Brand Awareness — broad topics, personality, opinions, entertainment (JABS)
- **Middle (20%):** Consideration — case studies, how-tos, expertise (deeper JABS)
- **Bottom (10%):** Conversion — direct offers, CTAs (RIGHT HOOKS)

Most businesses have this inverted. 70% sales, 20% educational, 10% personality. That's why nobody engages.

---

### 8. PERSONAL BRAND > COMPANY BRAND

- People trust people, not logos
- Founder posting personal content outperforms company page 10:1
- Share opinions. Be vulnerable. Take stands. Have a point of view.

---

### 9. PATIENCE + SPEED

**Micro speed:** Ship daily. Reply in minutes. Execute now. Don't wait for perfect.
**Macro patience:** Building a real brand takes 3-5 years minimum. Stop evaluating monthly.
