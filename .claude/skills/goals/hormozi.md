# Hormozi Frameworks Reference

Condensed from *$100M Offers* and *$100M Leads* by Alex Hormozi.
Used by the goals skill to build business action plans.

---

## 1. The Value Equation

> Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

To increase the perceived value of any offer, move these four levers:

| Lever | Direction | How |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Dream Outcome | Increase | Make the end state vivid and specific. "Get 3 clients" is weak. "Replace your salary in 90 days" is strong. |
| Perceived Likelihood | Increase | Add proof (case studies, testimonials, credentials). Add a guarantee. Show the mechanism. |
| Time Delay | Decrease | How fast do they see results? Compress the timeline or show quick wins. |
| Effort & Sacrifice | Decrease | What do they have to do vs. what do you handle? "Done with you" beats "figure it out yourself." |

## 2. The Grand Slam Offer

An offer no one can refuse because it solves the whole problem, not part of it.

**Structure:**
1. **Niche down** — pick a specific person with a specific problem. "Business owners" is too broad. "Coaches doing $5–20k/month who can't scale past 1:1" is a niche.
2. **Name the dream outcome** — use their words, not yours
3. **Stack the value** — list every piece of the solution (what you do, what they get, what problems each piece solves)
4. **Remove obstacles** — for each step they'd normally struggle with, show how you handle it
5. **Reverse the risk** — guarantee that removes their fear of loss

**The one-line formula:**
> "I help [specific person] get [dream outcome] in [timeframe] without [main obstacle]."

Examples:
- "I help e-commerce founders add $50k/month using paid ads without hiring a team."
- "I help solo consultants land 2 new clients per month without cold calls or a big audience."

## 3. Offer Positioning for Your Site

Every page of your site should answer one question at a time. The sequence:

| Section | Question It Answers | What to Write |
|---------|---------------------|---------------|
| Hero | "Is this for me?" | Your one-line offer + CTA (Book a Call / Apply Now) |
| Problem | "Do they get my situation?" | Agitate the exact pain. Use their words. |
| Solution | "How does this work?" | Your mechanism — the unique way you solve it |
| Proof | "Has this worked for others?" | 3–5 specific results with names/numbers |
| Offer | "What do I actually get?" | List deliverables, timeline, what's included |
| FAQ | "What's my objection?" | Handle the top 5 doubts directly |
| CTA | "What do I do next?" | One action only. Book a call or buy. |

**Rules:**
- One CTA per page. Don't give them choices.
- Proof before price. Never show price without showing results first.
- Specificity wins. "3 clients in 60 days" beats "grow your business."

## 4. Lead Generation System

Three channels that work without a big budget:

### Channel 1 — Outreach (fastest to revenue)
- Identify 20 ideal prospects per week
- Send a personalized first message (see templates in outreach-tracker.md)
- Follow up 3–4 times over 14 days
- Goal: book a discovery call, not make a sale on the first message

### Channel 2 — Content (builds inbound over time)
- Pick one platform where your ideal client already spends time
- Post 3–5x/week: one educational post, one opinion/POV, one case study or result
- Every piece of content should have a soft CTA ("DM me X if you want this")
- Repurpose: one long-form piece → 5 short-form posts

### Channel 3 — Referrals (highest close rate)
- After every client win, ask: "Who else do you know who'd benefit from this?"
- Create a simple referral incentive (commission, credit, gift)
- Build a list of strategic partners (complementary services, no competition)

**Lead gen math:**
- 20 outreach / week × 10% reply rate = 2 conversations
- 2 conversations × 50% call rate = 1 call/week
- 4 calls/month × 25% close rate = 1 new client/month
- Adjust volume to hit your target

## 5. The Funnel Framework

Stranger → Lead → Prospect → Client. Each stage has one job.

```
[Content / Outreach]
        ↓
[Lead Magnet] — gives value, captures email/DM
        ↓
[Nurture] — 3–5 emails or messages building trust + desire
        ↓
[VSL Page] — video sales letter that pre-sells before the call
        ↓
[Booking Page] — calendar link, application form
        ↓
[Discovery Call] — qualify, connect, present, close
        ↓
[Client]
```

**Each stage's single job:**
- Lead Magnet: exchange value for contact info
- Nurture: build belief (in you, in the outcome, in themselves)
- VSL: answer "why you, why now, why this" before the call
- Booking Page: filter out bad fits with a short application
- Discovery Call: close or disqualify

## 6. VSL Structure (8 Steps)

A video sales letter that does the selling before you ever get on a call.

1. **Hook** — pattern interrupt. "If you're a [type of person] struggling with [problem], this is the most important video you'll watch this year."
2. **Big Promise** — state the outcome up front. Don't make them wait.
3. **Credibility** — why you? One sentence. Numbers or a name they'd recognize.
4. **Problem Agitation** — describe their current pain in their words. Make them feel understood.
5. **Solution Reveal** — introduce your mechanism. Not the offer yet — the approach.
6. **Proof** — 2–3 specific results. Name + outcome + timeframe.
7. **Offer Stack** — reveal everything they get. Build value before revealing price.
8. **CTA** — one action. "Click the button below to book your free strategy call."

**VSL length:** 10–20 minutes for a high-ticket offer. Under 5 minutes for low-ticket.

## 7. Discovery Call Framework

Goal: qualify fast, connect deeply, present clearly, close confidently.

**Phase 1 — Qualify (5 min)**
- "Tell me a bit about what you're working on."
- "What's the main thing you're trying to solve right now?"
- [If not a fit: end gracefully and refer out]

**Phase 2 — Connect (10 min)**
- "How long has this been a problem?"
- "What have you tried already?"
- "What would it mean for you if you solved this in the next 90 days?"
- Listen more than you talk. Their words become your close.

**Phase 3 — Present (10 min)**
- "Based on what you've told me, here's what I'd do..." [walk through the offer]
- Tie each deliverable back to something they said in Phase 2
- Show proof relevant to their situation

**Phase 4 — Close (10 min)**
- "Does this feel like the right fit for where you are?"
- Handle objections one at a time: "I hear you — what would need to be true for this to make sense?"
- Common objections and responses:
  - "I need to think about it" → "What specifically do you need to think through? Let's work through it now."
  - "It's too expensive" → "Compared to what? What's it costing you to stay where you are?"
  - "I need to talk to my partner" → "If your partner is supportive, are you in?" [get conditional yes first]

## 8. Outreach Principles

- **Personalize the first line** — reference something specific. Generic = ignored.
- **Lead with curiosity, not a pitch** — ask a question or make an observation. Don't sell in the first message.
- **One ask per message** — the ask is always: are you open to a conversation? Not: buy my thing.
- **Follow up without apology** — most sales happen on follow-up 3–5. Don't stop after one try.
- **The breakup message converts** — a genuine "last message, no hard feelings" often gets a reply.
- **Volume is a lever** — if close rate is fine but pipeline is thin, increase outreach volume, not pitch quality.

## Key Numbers to Track

| Metric | Target | What to Do If Low |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Outreach sent / week | 20+ | Block time for it |
| Reply rate | >10% | Improve first message personalization |
| Call booking rate | >50% of replies | Improve follow-up sequence |
| Call close rate | >25% | Improve discovery call / offer clarity |
| Client LTV | [your target] | Add upsells, retainers, or referral incentive |
