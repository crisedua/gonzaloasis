## FRAMEWORK LIBRARY

### 1. THE VALUE EQUATION

**Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)**

This is the lens through which you evaluate EVERYTHING.

**Dream Outcome** — The emotional experience and tangible result the customer envisions when their problem is solved. Not features. Not deliverables. What does their life LOOK like after? Sell the vacation, not the plane ticket.

**Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** — How confident the prospect is that THIS solution will work for THEM. Increase through: social proof, demonstrated authority, specificity of the promise, and guarantees.

**Time Delay** — How long before they see the first result. Not the final result. The FIRST win. The faster you can deliver the first tangible result, the more valuable you become.

**Effort & Sacrifice** — What does the customer have to give up or do to get the result? Reduce this toward zero. Done-for-you > done-with-you > do-it-yourself.

**Critical insight:** Beginners focus on the top of the equation (bigger promises). The real competitive advantage is in the BOTTOM. If you can get time delay and effort to near zero, value approaches infinity.

### 2. THE GRAND SLAM OFFER (Complete Build Process)

**Step 1: Pick a Starving Crowd**
Four indicators (ALL four required):
- **Massive Pain** — They desperately need what you sell
- **Purchasing Power** — They can afford your price
- **Easy to Target** — You can find them through associations, lists, communities
- **Growing** — A tailwind is pushing in your direction

**Step 2: Charge Premium Prices**
Higher prices create a virtuous cycle: higher perceived value, more committed clients, better results, more profit to reinvest, less competition.

**Step 3: Create the Value Stack**
- 3a. Identify the Dream Outcome
- 3b. List ALL problems (before, during, and after purchasing)
- 3c. Transform problems into solutions
- 3d. Determine delivery vehicles (6 dimensions: attention level, effort level, support medium, format, speed, ongoing vs one-time)
- 3e. Trim: remove high-cost/low-value items. Keep high-value/low-cost
- 3f. Stack and name each component: Problem -> Solution -> Sexy Bundle Name

**10x Test:** "What would I provide if customers paid 10x?"
**1/10th Test:** "How would I deliver if they paid 1/10th and I still had to overdeliver?"

**Step 4: Enhance with Guarantees**
Types (least to most aggressive):
- Unconditional: Full refund, no questions
- Conditional: Money back IF specific conditions aren't met
- Stacked: Unconditional + conditional combined
- Anti-Guarantee: "All sales final" with compelling reason
- Performance: Pay-per-result, rev share, milestone payments

**Step 5: Add Real Scarcity & Urgency**
- Scarcity = limited quantity (cohort caps, finite spots)
- Urgency = limited time (enrollment windows, price increases)
- Both must be REAL. Fake scarcity destroys trust permanently.

**Step 6: Name It with MAGIC**
- M = Magnet word (dream outcome: "Instant," "Rapid," "Effortless")
- A = Avatar (who: "Busy Moms," "SaaS Founders")
- G = Goal (what: "Six-Pack Abs," "$10K Months")
- I = Interval (when: "30-Day," "8-Week")
- C = Container (what it is: "Challenge," "Bootcamp," "System")

### 3. THE CORE FOUR (From $100M Leads)

The only four ways to let people know about what you sell:

**1. Warm Outreach** (1:1 to people who know you)
- ACA Framework: Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask
- Fastest path to first 5 clients. Zero cost.

**2. Post Free Content** (1:many to warm audience)
- Formula: Hook -> Retain -> Reward
- Give until they ask. Provide so much free value they think "If this is free, what's the paid stuff like?"

**3. Cold Outreach** (1:1 to strangers)
- Three elements: Lists, Personalization, Big Fast Value, Volume
- Lead with massive value, not a pitch

**4. Paid Ads** (1:many to cold audience)
- 3 W's: What are you offering? Why should they care? When do they need to act?
- Client-Financed Acquisition: frontend payments fund ad spend, backend is pure profit

### 4. LEAD MAGNETS

Rules:
- Solve a narrow but significant problem completely
- Reveal a BIGGER problem your paid offer solves
- Must be immediately consumable (under 15 minutes)
- Must provide a "wow" moment
- Value should EXCEED the cost of your core offer in the prospect's mind

### 5. THE RULE OF 100

Whatever method you choose:
- Warm/Cold Outreach: 100 reach-outs per day
- Content: 100 minutes per day creating
- Paid Ads: $100/day minimum

Do this for 100 days without stopping. Most people quit on day 14.
